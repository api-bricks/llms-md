Markets Move and Your Candles Should Too

Crypto markets don't organize themselves into our time boxes. They move trade by trade‚Ä¶ tick-by-tick. While you're staring at a 15-minute candle forming every trade can:

* Push to a new high point
* Drop to a new low
* Change the current closing price
* Add to the volume count

A candle at minute 3 could look completely different by minute 10‚Ä¶ and **totally flip by the close‚Ä¶** And each shift tells you something about market behavior.

Real-time vs. Delayed
---------------------

Currently we can see two approaches:

1. **Traditional approach**: You wait for the candle to close

‚úîÔ∏è Clean, structured data

‚ùå But‚Ä¶ you‚Äôre always reacting to *old info*

2. **Real-time approach**: You watch the market shift in real-time

‚úîÔ∏è You see moves *as they happen*

‚ùå Requires more focus‚Ä¶ but also gives you an advantage

For active traders, this gap isn‚Äôt just theoretical. No. It‚Äôs the difference between **catching the move and watching it happen too late.**

Most traders focus on final candles‚Ä¶ but the real insights come from watching them form:

* A sudden volume spike early in a candle? Smart money could be making a move‚Ä¶
* A failed breakout before period closes? That‚Äôs resistance in action‚Ä¶.
* A price rejection mid-candle? Market sentiment shifting in real-time‚Ä¶

These signals disappear if you only see the final candle.

Different Styles... and Different Needs
---------------------------------------

Your approach to crypto OHLCV data should always match your trading style:

* Scalpers - need to see candles evolve in real time. Every second counts!
* Swing traders - can work with period-close data. Timing matters‚Ä¶ but not by the second.
* Algo traders - thrive on intra-period data. Every micro-move feeds their strategy.

**Your data should adapt to your style not the other way around.**
